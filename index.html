Welcome to Library Accessibility Services

Library Accessibility Services (LAS) needs your feedback. We're looking to make improvements to our physical and digital services, to ensure that they are fitting your needs. We want to make changes that are informed by you. If you could, please take five minutes and answer these two surveys, which will give us much appreciated data on how to move forward.

Adaptive Technology Centre (ATC) Survey: Is our space working for you? Have you used the ATC and it's resources? If so, how did you like them? If not, why not? Take a few minutes and let us know how we can better serve you in our lab space.

LAS Website Survey: This very webiste needs an update, but we want to make sure the changes make it a better experience for you, our users. Take a few minutes to let us know what improvements could be made to our digital home.

We are committed to accommodating diverse student accessibility needs.

Enjoy an accessible, scent-free environment at the Adaptive Technology Centre, room 251, a designated Quiet Zone on the main floor of the Dana Porter Library.

Our accessibility services, technologies, and equipment can help enhance your educational experience.

The University of Waterloo acknowledges that much of our work takes place on the traditional territory of the Neutral, Anishinaabeg and Haudenosaunee peoples. Our main campus is situated on the Haldimand Tract, the land granted to the Six Nations that includes six miles on each side of the Grand River. Our active work toward reconciliation takes place across our campuses through research, learning, teaching, and community building, and is co-ordinated within the Office of Indigenous Relations.
